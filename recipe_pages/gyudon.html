Gyudon

Description

Gyudon, otherwise known as the Japanese beef bowl, is a popular, easy-to-make comfort food, containing thinly sliced beef and tender onions in a savory sauce, all over a warm bowl of rice.
Various additions such as eggs and pickled ginger could be added to this nutritious food for more flavor, allowing for many different eating experiences.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 sliced onion
  • 1 scallion
  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced beef
  • 1/2 cup dashi
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Rice

Steps

  1. Cook rice. Serve when ready.
  2. Cut the sliced beef into smaller 3 in. pieces.
  3. Using a large pan, put in the dashi, sake, mirin, soy sauce, and sugar. Mix to combine.
  4. Add in the onion, making sure to separate the layers.
  5. Add in the meat.
  6. Cover the pan and let the ingredients cook over medium heat. As the meat browns, skim off the scum that forms.
  7. Turn the heat down to low once the meat is thoroughly brown and simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  8. Add scallion once finished cooking.
  9. Serve a bowl of rice and add the cooked meat on top.
