Shanghai Style Braised Pork Belly

Description

Shanghai style braised pork belly is a well-known famous dish throughout China, known for its deep red color as well as its tenderness.
The pork belly is cooked in such a way that makes it more gelatinous, allowing the meat to melt in the mouth.

Ingredients

  • Pork Belly
  • Shaoxing Rice Wine
  • Dark and Light Soy Sauce
  • Ginger
  • Star-anise
  • Bay Leaf
  • Rock Sugar
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Cut pork belly into 3/4 inch thick pieces.
  2. Blanch the cut pork belly in boiling water for a few minutes. Remove and set aside pork belly.
  3. Using a wok on low heat, add oil and sugar.
  4. Once the sugar is partially melted, turn the heat to medium and add the pork belly. Cook until pork belly is lightly brown.
  5. Lower heat to low and add Shaoxing cooking wine, water, spices, and both soy sauces.
  6. Cover and let the meat simmer for 45 - 60 minutes. Stir every 5 - 10 minutes to prevent burning. Add more water when necessary.
  7. Once the meat is tender, turn up the heat and reduce the sauce.
